Solid Likely Needs review

Dot shape carries meaning (disc / half / ring) so color is never the sole signal. Muted fills keep confidence-green off the brand-win green.

Option 2 · dot only

Compact, but fails color-blind and VoiceOver users: meaning lives only in hue. Rejected.

Why option 1

Shape + text label is the only treatment that satisfies the spec's "tiers not percentages" and the a11y floor at once. One primitive, reused on KPI cards, category rows, habit cards, and projection lines.

SpendPulse · coverage honesty

Zero-spend and out-of-coverage MUST look different. Out-of-coverage is hatched.

Final

Chosen · heat ramp + neutral + hatch

more spent no spend outside your files

You transacted on 14 of 21 days. Red is a heat ramp (the one sanctioned danger-hue use), neutral is a covered zero-spend day, hatch is no data.

Pros

  • Three fills are unmistakable: ramp vs flat neutral vs hatch
  • Hatch reads "no data" even at 1-bit / grayscale
  • Shares grid grammar with the habit calendar but never its day-states (different fills + labeled legend)

Cons / guardrail

  • Red ramp must never be misread as a slip: legend label says "more spent", not "bad"
  • Hatch density needs testing at year-view 7×53 micro-scale

Why no red

  • Three ordered exits mean the flow never dead-ends (it degrades into Door 1)
  • Same calm tokens as the rest; only the copy carries the apology
