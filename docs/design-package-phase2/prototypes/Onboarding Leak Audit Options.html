5a · The reveal

Pros

  • Fastest of the three: ~10 taps, no keyboard ever appears
  • Running total moves with every tap, the estimate feels alive
  • Bands are honest: imprecise inputs framed as imprecise
  • Chips are 44pt+ targets, best VoiceOver story
  • Each chip seeds one recurring expense at its preset value

Cons

  • Preset prices will be wrong for some users (edit is a tap away, but later)
  • Band midpoints hide the math (3-5 counts as 4)
  • "Something else" still needs a typed amount

Tradeoff

Speed over precision. Right call for a 90-second gate: precision arrives later from real logs, completion does not get a second chance. Best funnel odds of the three.

Recommendation: Option A, with B's inline amount edit folded in

A is the only option that clears the 90-second gate with margin (~10 taps, no keyboard), keeps every input honest (bands framed as bands, tilde everywhere), seeds one recurring expense per chip, and has the cleanest funnel and VoiceOver story. Fold in the one thing B does better: tapping the price on a selected chip opens an inline edit, so users who know their exact number can set it without slowing anyone else down. Steal C's liveliness: the running total animates with every tap.

  • Per-step funnel: onboarding_started → door_chosen → audit_subs_done → audit_vices_done → leak_revealed → first_log_saved → onboarding_completed (names map to the pre-declared events in analytics.ts)
  • Skippable at every step, no dead ends: skipping both estimate steps routes to the guided log with the reveal omitted, not a zero
  • Door 2 graceful failure re-enters this flow with anything the scan found pre-selected as chips

E1 · After a slip: the week absorbs it

Pros

  • Nothing to "break": a slip costs one dot, not a 14-day identity
  • Matches the research: habit formation survives missed days, streaks don't
  • "3 of 4 days" is honest and always recoverable this week
  • Week strip doubles as a mini calendar, less UI than streak + calendar

Cons

  • Loses the streak's compulsion loop, the strongest known retention driver
  • Milestones (7, 14, 30, 66 in a row) need reframing to totals
  • Long-timers lose bragging rights ("212 days" means something)

Tradeoff

Retention hook vs guilt. Trades the streak's grip for slip-safety. For an app whose promise is "no judgment", the streak was always the contradiction. Milestones become "50 total skips", which still compounds.

E2 · Chapters on the detail screen

Pros

  • Progress only accumulates: structurally guilt-free
  • 66-skip arc has a real finish line ("rewired"), streaks never end
  • Identity framing ("you're becoming...") is the strongest habit-change lever in the literature
  • Coach Moments slot in naturally as chapter narration

Cons

  • Abstract: a ring and chapters need explaining, a streak doesn't
  • "Rewired" at 66 is a claim; some habits take longer, copy must hedge
  • Risk of feeling gamified or precious for a money app
  • Daily urgency is weaker: nothing is lost by waiting

Tradeoff

Meaning vs immediacy. Deepest guilt reduction and the best story, but the least daily pull. Works best as the detail-screen layer on top of E1's card, not as the daily interface itself.

E3 · Skip feedback: the level rises

Pros

  • Most visceral reward: watching money accumulate beats reading a number
  • Jar is win-only, so the daily surface carries zero guilt by construction
  • Instantly explicable, no onboarding needed
  • Screenshot-shareable, an organic growth surface

Cons

  • A jar needs a top: what does "full" mean? Requires a target or it reads arbitrary
  • Illustration-heavy: biggest lift and biggest style risk in the current flat system
  • Hiding slips from the main view risks the C-honesty principle if overdone
  • Scales poorly across multiple habits (one jar each? one shared?)

Tradeoff

Delight vs honesty and scale. The strongest single moment (coin drops in) but the weakest system: needs a fill target, an illustration language, and a multi-habit answer. Steal the drop-in moment as the Kept hero's count-up flourish rather than adopting the whole jar.

Recommendation: E1 card + E2 detail layer, steal E3's moment

Replace the streak chip with E1's week strip on the check-in card: it keeps daily engagement but makes a slip cost one dot instead of an identity. Put E2's 66-skip arc on the detail screen as the long-term narrative (total skips, never subtracting), which also gives milestones a finish line. Fold E3 down to its best moment: the +$6.00 count-up on the Kept hero gets a brief fill-pulse so the win lands physically. This combination addresses guilt at all three time scales: today (two honest buttons, slip absorbed by the week), this week (rhythm, not perfection), and the long arc (progress that only accumulates).

  • Data model unchanged: skip/slip events, Kept totals, and the 3-state calendar all stay as specced in 01
  • Copy changes: streak strings become "n of m days this week" and "n skips toward rewired"
  • If you want to keep streaks: E1 can carry the streak as a quiet secondary stat on detail only, out of the daily loop

F3 · The reveal, unchanged

Step goals and the handoff into the app

  • Step 1, auto-pilot charges. Goal: recognition of the quiet recurring suspects, never an inventory. Each tap seeds one recurring expense the app watches in Upcoming.
  • Step 2, everyday rhythm. Goal: frequency bands for the impulse leaks. This is the behavioral half of the estimate and what detection will test first.
  • Reveal, the hypothesis. "~$2,930 a year" is framed as a starting estimate, tilde mandatory. Its biggest line becomes the suggested first habit.
  • First log, then landing. One practiced log teaches the daily mechanic. Success lands on the Habits tab: leak candidates listed, Kept at zero, counter primed.

Copy principle: ask what rings a bell, never "enter everything". About 10 taps end to end.

Behavior rules

  • Preset prices show a tilde; edited prices drop it and show green underline, signalling "yours"
  • Tapping a price expands the chip to a full-width editor: amount preselected on a numeric keyboard, Set commits, ✕ cancels back to the preset; a caption shows the preset so you can always back out
  • Edited chips seed recurring expenses at the exact value; untouched chips seed at preset
  • Editing never adds a screen or a required step: pure fast-path A if ignored
  • Analytics: audit_amount_edited {step, count} fires per commit, structural only

Why this is final

Keeps A's ~10-tap, no-keyboard fast path for the majority while giving precision-minded users B's exactness exactly where they want it. C's liveliness survives as the animating running total. Spec to follow in 02-p2-1-onboarding-leak-audit.md.
